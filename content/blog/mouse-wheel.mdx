The other day, my mouse died.

I was using it like normal when suddenly I couldn't scroll anymore. At first, I thought my battery died. I've had my Logitech G703 for around 4 years now and it's served me well in that time, but it was beginning to show its age, demanding to be charged at least twice a week. But... everything else was still working. I could move my cursor, left click, right click, side buttons, even the middle click still worked. Just no more scrolling. 

I'm not really a mouse guy, I don't care about them nearly as much as I do with keyboards. If it can move my cursor and feels nice to hold - I'm happy. But as someone that spends a lot of time in front of my computer, not having a functional scroll wheel was going to be a problem. I asked my mouse nerd friend for some recommendations and just picked whichever one had the closest shape to the G703.

A few hours after clicking buy, I suddenly remembered that I have a 3D printer! What if the mouse's scroll wheel breaking wasn't a hardware issue (i.e. scroll sensor failure) but the result of a mechanical part wearing down over the years? Doesn't hurt to try, the worst that could happen is my already broken mouse... remains broken. 

Unfortunately, there weren't any existing mouse wheel models for the G703 that I liked, so I downloaded [this](https://makerworld.com/en/models/2416274-scroll-wheel-for-logitech-g-pro-x-superlight?from=search#profileId-2649612) model for the Logitech Superlight. Although it's for a different mouse, the Superlight is similar in size to the G703 and the model was designed with a separate wheel and shaft which would make it easy to modify and fast to iterate on. 

As expected, the printed mouse wheel did not fit.  Specifically, the G703's shaft is a completely different size to the Superlight. But that's nothing a craft knife and some sandpaper can't fix!

![The wheel for the Superlight is smaller and too narrow for the G703](blog/mouse-2.png)

And it worked! The mouse was scrolling again. It was a little janky and nowhere near as smooth as the original scroll wheel but the sensor was indeed fine. It was mechanical all along. Too bad I had already bought a replacement mouse 😛

Honestly, I'm not certain what the problem even was. There didn't seem to be anything visually wrong with the mouse wheel. I suspect the portion of the shaft that interfaces with the scroll sensor rounded out over time and eventually became so rounded out it just became a free spinning wheel. Either way, replacing it fixed whatever the problem was.

Now I just had to modify the model to actually fit the G703.

![Big fan of calipers :)](blog/mouse-5.png)

And a few iterations, we have a printable replacement mousewheel for the G703 (and G403/G603)!

![The G703 mouse wheel shaft features a slightly larger wheel diameter and width, and thinner shaft with different proportions](blog/mouse-1.png)

Thank you to mr user_2572770550 for creating this. I will upload my remixed model here later to be available for download. 

> **Note:** The tolerances are pretty tight so you will probably need to fiddle with it a little bit so the wheel doesn't catch on the sides. Mine scrolls perfectly now but it took some trial and error. I also used some superglue to keep the two pieces locked in place in case there was any lateral wiggling.
